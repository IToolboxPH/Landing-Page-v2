## 🎯 Why Keep Them Separate?

### 1. Different Purposes
- IToolbox serves business needs
- OnePath serves spiritual needs
- Mixing them dilutes both missions

### 2. Different Audiences
- IToolbox: Business decision-makers
- OnePath: Faith seekers and believers
- Different messaging and approach needed

### 3. Credibility
- Faith initiative shouldn't appear profit-driven
- Business shouldn't be seen as religious organization
- Each has distinct value proposition

### 4. Legal & Tax Considerations
- Commercial products are taxable business
- Faith donations may have different treatment
- Clear separation protects both entities

### 5. Operational Focus
- IToolbox team focuses on product development
- OnePath can operate with volunteer model
- Different priorities and timelines

## 🎗️ Best Practices

### Time Management
- **IToolbox:** Primary focus, business hours, team collaboration
- **OnePath:** Secondary focus, personal time, volunteer-driven
- Don't let OnePath distract from IToolbox success
- Don't let IToolbox pressure compromise OnePath mission

### Financial Separation
- **IToolbox:** Business bank account, revenue tracking
- **OnePath:** Separate donations account (if/when needed)
- Never mix commercial and faith-based funds
- Full transparency for OnePath donations

### Messaging Consistency
- Be clear about which hat you're wearing
- Sign IToolbox communications as "CEO"
- Sign OnePath communications as "Founder" or "Brother in Christ"
- Make distinction clear in public appearances

### Team Clarity (Future)
- IToolbox team works on commercial products
- OnePath is founder's personal initiative
- Team members can volunteer for OnePath
- But OnePath is not an IToolbox assignment
